— Project Overview

A Genomic Archive of Egyptian Y-DNA and mtDNA Lineages

The Egyptian Y-DNA Project is an independent initiative dedicated to documenting and organizing both the paternal and maternal genetic lineages of Egyptians in a clear, structured, and evidence-based way.

The archive now includes both a dedicated Y-DNA Tree and a dedicated mtDNA Tree, allowing visitors to explore Egyptian paternal and maternal lineages side by side. The mtDNA Tree is organized around major maternal branches, while both trees continue to grow as additional modern, study, and ancient samples are added over time.

By collecting real test results and presenting them in a neutral and scientific format, the project aims to serve as a professional genomic reference that reflects the diversity, continuity, and historical depth of the Egyptian population.
